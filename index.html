In my books and papers, I focus on questions that figure both in ancient and contemporary discussions: What are values? What kind of values are knowledge and truth? What is the role of epistemic norms in ethics? In two recent papers, I argue that Plato’s Euthyphro offers a distinctive metaethics (“The Euthyphro Problem Revisited” 2020) and philosophy of law (“Metaethics of Discord” forthcoming 2022) that integrate dimensions of realism and anti-realism. And I’m returning to themes in Stoic logic (“Vagueness and Kataleptic Impressions” 2022, “Stoic Definitions Without Essences” forthcoming, “The Flaw of Generics” forthcoming), physics (“The Agency of the World” 2021) and ethics (“The Stoic Conception of Law” 2021) and Pyrrho’s philosophy (“Pyrrhonian Indeterminacy” 2021).

In Desiring the Good: Ancient Proposals and Contemporary Theory (OUP, 2017, paperback 2021), I aim to show in which sense it may be true that human beings are “the measure” such that this is not a relativist proposal, but an insight about the nature of ethics. In Imagination and Agency, I aim to clarify the Aristotelian dictum that deliberation is about the possible, and what it means that some decision is right (“Deliberative Imagination and Practical Truth” forthcoming). I co-authored four papers with Jens Haas, “Incomplete Ignorance” (2020), “Ignorance and Investigation” (2015 and 2022), “Love and Hatred” (2018), and “Good and Evil?” (2022). In Epistemology After Sextus Empiricus (OUP, 2020), co-edited with Justin Vlasits, I return to my long-term interest in skepticism, and I’m finalizing a set of papers for a monograph, tentatively entitled The Original Stoics.

I serve as an editor of Nous, on the editorial boards of the Journal of Philosophy, Apeiron, and Rhizomata, as an associate editor for philosophy of language, epistemology, and logic of Dialogoi: Ancient Philosophy Today, and as one of the two North American co-editors of the Archiv für Geschichte der Philosophie. At Columbia University and due to the pandemic, I became interested in the opportunities and challenges of teaching and conducting research remotely. I’m chairing a subcommittee of the Policy and Planning Committee on Teaching Modalities and I’m an academic sponsor of Logic Made Accessible, a project devoted to making ancient logic freely accessible for students of all ages.

When I’m not in NYC, I’m traveling with photographer and artist Jens Haas. On the Road has become an art project and a philosophical diary. Movies and Morals, in collaboration with H8URS, is a growing series of video commentary on philosophy and art in contemporary film and television shows.
